## Hypothesis (built on R11, not blind)

R11 showed the soft-router *mechanism* works where regimes separate (SOL: only
positive DSR, +0.370). Bottleneck = a *per-asset* HMM on [own return, own vol]
degenerated on BTC/ETH. R12 changed the regime signal:
1. Cross-asset **market-structure** features instead of per-asset returns+vol.
2. **One shared regime** learned at market level, applied to all three — so
   BTC/ETH can't degenerate independently; they inherit the market regime.

If BTC/ETH inherited a separated regime, their combo DSR might turn positive
like SOL's and clear the gate.

## §3 Verdict — final HMM-direction conclusion

**R12 FAIL (0/3); registered as global trial #8. The HMM-regime direction is
closed.**

Why cross-asset sharing *hurt*: SOL's R11 edge was **idiosyncratic to SOL's own
regime** (its high-dispersion bull runs separate cleanly), not a market-wide
phenomenon. The market basket regime is dominated by BTC/ETH dynamics, which do
not separate; imposing it on SOL removed SOL's specific signal. Sharing a regime
cannot manufacture separation that isn't there — it averages toward the
worst-separating assets.

**Two attempts, one conclusion:**
- R11 (per-asset return+vol): degenerate on BTC/ETH; works only on SOL.
- R12 (cross-asset market structure): no OOS persistence; drags SOL down too.

HMM regime routing on crypto **majors** tops out: regimes here are not separable
the way the literature's SPY regimes are (paper Sharpe 1.22). The soft-router
*architecture* is sound (SOL/R11 proved it), but it is bottlenecked by a regime
classifier crypto majors won't support with the available data.

**Caveat that keeps the door honest:** this verdict is on OHLCV-derived features.
Real funding-rate and true-dominance history (unavailable here) might separate
crypto regimes better — that is the *only* remaining untested lever, and it needs
external data, not another HMM variant. Absent that data, HMM regime work stops.
