## §6 Verdict

### **NO-GO** — cost-killed before reaching walk-forward gate

All 9 strategy-instrument combinations are eliminated by the §3 cost sensitivity check.
No strategy achieves positive Sharpe at 15bps round-trip (OKX taker + conservative slippage).

### Why cost kills this alpha

The fundamental arithmetic:

| Strategy | Trades/day (BTC) | Cost @10bps RT/day | Cost @10bps annualized | Gross Sharpe needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| VWAP-MR | 8.5 | 8.5 × 0.10% = **0.85%/day** | ≈ **310% /year** | >> 1.33 |
| VolSurge | 11.2 | 11.2 × 0.10% = **1.12%/day** | ≈ **409% /year** | >> 0 |
| BB-Squeeze | 6.7 | 6.7 × 0.10% = **0.67%/day** | ≈ **245% /year** | >> 0 |

The VWAP-MR strategy has GENUINE gross alpha on BTC (+1.33 Sharpe at 0bps). This confirms
that 5m VWAP deviation is a real microstructure signal. But the edge per trade is ~10-20bps
while the cost per round-trip is 10bps standard (20bps with slippage). The strategy needs
to earn back its entire cost on EVERY trade just to break even. Actual per-trade P&L is
far smaller than this (Sharpe +1.33 at 8.5 trades/day with hold=12 bars ≈ 0.5-2% move).

### The core constraint: taker fee is ~equal to signal edge at 5m

5m scalping lives in a regime where the cost-per-trade ≈ the expected profit-per-trade.
This is NOT a strategy calibration issue — no parameter tuning will fix this.
It requires one of:
1. **Maker rebate**: post limit orders at the bid/ask → 0bps or negative fee (rebate)
2. **Fewer, larger-edge trades**: 30m-1H holding, fewer entries — but then it's not scalping
3. **L2 order book signal**: smarter entry timing to reduce adverse selection
