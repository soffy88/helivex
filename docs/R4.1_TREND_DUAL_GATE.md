## §2 Strategy

- **Signal**: Donchian Channel dual-direction (close-based)
  - Long when `close[i] > max(close[i-20 : i])` — new 20-bar close high → enter long
  - Short when `close[i] < min(close[i-20 : i])` — new 20-bar close low → enter short
  - Flat when inside the channel (no breakout)
- **Exit**: 10-bar close channel — long exits when `close[i] < min(close[i-10 : i])`; short when above exit channel high
- **Costs**: OKX taker 5bps/leg; funding prorated per 4H bar (avg 0.0014% per 8H for BTC)
- **No look-ahead**: channel uses `range(i-N, i)` — bars strictly BEFORE bar i; close[i] CAN exceed the prior-bar channel ✓

## §7 Verdict

**Gate: FAIL (0/3). Root cause: chop, not a broken dual-direction hypothesis.**

Segment analysis reveals a clear structure:

| Regime | BTC Sharpe | ETH Sharpe | SOL Sharpe | % of bars |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bull (5-day return > +5%) | **+11.0** | **+6.2** | **+3.3** | ~10% |
| Bear (5-day return < −5%) | **+9.8** | **+7.1** | **+6.7** | ~15% |
| Chop (between ±5%) | **−5.1** | **−5.5** | **−8.0** | **~75%** |

**The dual-direction hypothesis is validated:** both bull and bear regimes show strongly positive Sharpe ratios (the short leg earns in the bear phase exactly as predicted). The strategy is profitable in 25% of time but gets whipsawed for 75% of time in the choppy regime, wiping out all gains.

**Why the gate fails despite trend alpha:** With 5-fold CPCV, which fold sees trending vs choppy bars is determined by the time-split, not the strategy. The fold with the best IS Sharpe happens to land on a trending period; its OOS counterpart lands on chop → PBO=1.0 (IS-best = OOS-worst). The gate correctly identifies that trend quality is regime-dependent and the regime is not predictable within this framework.

**Path forward: R4.2 — add chop filter.**
Options:
- ADX (Average Directional Index): trade only when ADX > 25 (trend strength)
- ATR filter: trade only when recent ATR > N-period average (high-vol = trending)
- Regime gate: 100-bar SMA slope → trade only in direction of prevailing trend
