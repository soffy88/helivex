## §2 R5.1 Cost Math Verification

**Q: "8.5 trades/day" = 8.5 round-trips or 8.5 single legs?**

Answer: **8.5 complete round-trips** (entry + exit pairs).
`_sim_scalp` increments `n_trades` once at entry only. Every entry has exactly
one timed exit after `hold` bars. So 8.5 trades/day = 8.5 full entries = 17 single-leg orders.

**Q: Is 10bps round-trip or one-way?**

Answer: **10bps is the full round-trip** (entry + exit combined).
Code: `cost_oneway = cost_bps / 20000`. Both entry and exit charge `cost_oneway`.
Total per trade = 2 × cost_oneway = cost_bps / 10000. At cost_bps=10: 0.1% per round-trip.

**Q: OKX actual taker fee?**

OKX USDT perpetual swap (Tier 1 / 普通用户):
- Taker: 0.05%/side = **5bps/side = 10bps round-trip** ✓ matches R5.1 model
- Maker: 0.02%/side = **2bps/side = 4bps round-trip** (R5.2 target)
- VIP maker rebate: up to −0.005%/side for top tiers (modeled as −5bps RT here)

## §7 Look-Ahead Audit

- VWAP signals: `.shift(1)` rolling windows → signals[i] uses bars [0..i-1] only ✓
- Limit entry at close[i]: close[i] is known at bar i end → no look-ahead ✓
- Fill check: high[i+1] >= close[i] uses bar i+1 data ONLY to confirm fill ✓
- 1-bar patience: cancel if not filled in bar i+1 → no peeking at future fill ✓

## §8 Verdict

### **NO-GO — maker execution insufficient to rescue signal**

### Why maker doesn't help: 97% fill rate = no selectivity

The fill rate at close[i] limit is **97%**. In liquid 5m crypto futures, consecutive 5m bars
have negligible gap (close[i] ≈ open[i+1]), and high[i+1] ≥ open[i+1] always. The "limit"
at close[i] is effectively a delayed market order. There is almost no trade selectivity.

### The structural constraint: trade frequency, not just cost per trade

| Model | Trades/day | Cost/trade | Daily cost | Annualized |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Taker (R5.1) | 8.4 | 10bps RT | 0.84% | 307%/yr |
| Maker (Tier 1) | 8.3 | 4bps RT | 0.33% | 121%/yr |
| Maker (rebate -5bps) | 8.3 | -5bps RT | −0.42% | −152%/yr |

Even at maker rates (4bps RT), the 8.3-trade/day frequency imposes **121% annualized cost drag**.
The gross Sharpe (0bps) is +0.509 (maker) vs +0.813 (taker) — the 1-bar fill delay slightly
hurts the mean reversion alpha. Neither can withstand realistic fee rates.

**Only viable at rebate (~−5bps RT):** +3.822 gross Sharpe IS achievable at −5bps.
This requires VIP or market-maker tier (not 普通用户). Not a realistic path.

### Root cause: edge-per-trade ≈ fee-per-trade at 5m

The VWAP-MR alpha at 5m bar resolution earns ~0.3-0.8bps per bar of hold over 12 bars =
~4-10bps per trade at 0bps gross. Cost per trade (taker = 10bps, maker = 4bps) consumes
the entire or majority of the per-trade P&L. This is a structural regime problem, not tunable.

### Next step

No viable 5m scalping path at any standard fee tier. Possible future angles:
1. **30m-1H VWAP mean reversion**: 4× longer hold, ~2×/day trades → cost burden drops to ~30%/yr at taker, trivially viable at maker
2. **L2 order book data**: smarter entry timing to capture spread (requires non-OHLCV data)
3. **Market-making VIP tier**: −5bps rebate makes current strategy work, but requires large notional
