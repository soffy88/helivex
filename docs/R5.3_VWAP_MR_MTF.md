# R5.3 VWAP Mean Reversion — Multi-Timeframe (30m / 1H)

> Hypothesis: 5m VWAP-MR has genuine gross alpha but 8.4 trades/day × 10bps taker = 307%/yr kills it.
> 30m/1H: same signal, fewer trades, larger P&L/trade → cost burden ~30-70%/yr → net alpha?
> Gate: taker 10bps RT (no maker assumption). Same DSR+PBO thresholds as all prior gates.

## §0 Parameter Design Note

VWAP window must match the **same absolute time period** across timeframes, not the same bar count.
5m uses vwap_n=48 × 5m = 240 min = **4H**. The 30m/1H equivalents:

| Timeframe | Bars | Absolute window | Hold bars | Hold (absolute) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5m  | 48 | 4H | 12 | 1H |
| 30m |  8 | 4H | 12 | 6H |
| 1H  |  4 | 4H |  6 | 6H |

Using a 24H or 48H VWAP window at 30m/1H is a **different signal** (daily VWAP deviation, not
intraday). Naive bar-count parity (vwap_n=48 for all TFs) destroys the alpha mechanism
and shows negative gross Sharpe — not because the mechanism fails, but because it tests
a different signal. All results below use the 4H-equivalent window.

## §6 Look-Ahead Audit

- VWAP rolling windows use `.shift(1)`: signals[i] uses bars [0..i-1] only ✓
- 30m/1H bars resampled from 5m: aggregation uses only confirmed closed 5m bars ✓
- Resampling: open=first, high=max, low=min, close=last, volume=sum over correct window ✓
- Entry at bar i+1 open ≈ bar i close (1-bar lag); no future data in signal computation ✓
- Context prepend: last 100 IS bars for warmup; fresh pos=0 at test start ✓
- Per-cycle sharpe: signals computed on FULL instrument array, sliced by date — no future leak ✓

## §7 Verdict

### **NO-GO — gate fails despite positive @10bps gross (SOL 1H)**

### Hypothesis evaluation: frequency reduction partially works

| Claim | Result |
|---|---|
| Reducing frequency cuts cost burden | ✓ — 307%/yr → 104%/yr (3× reduction) |
| Gross alpha survives at 30m/1H | ✓ — with 4H-equivalent window (not 24H naive) |
| Net alpha positive at taker (10bps) | Barely: SOL 1H = +0.074, all others negative |
| Gate passes (DSR+PBO) | ✗ — SOL 1H: DSR=-1.436, PBO=0.86 |

### Why SOL 1H fails the gate despite positive @10bps Sharpe

SOL 1H OOS fold returns: [+0.888, −0.889, −0.213, −1.169, +0.648, +0.910, +0.283, −0.276]

The fold-level variance is extreme. The DSR correction penalizes this volatility in OOS results:
a mean OOS Sharpe of +0.023 with this much dispersion → adjusted Sharpe goes deeply negative
under multiple-comparisons correction. The strategy is not **consistently** positive across folds;
it is sometimes excellent and sometimes severely negative.

### Per-cycle pattern: works in volatility extremes, fails in trends

SOL 1H performance by regime:

| Works ✓ | Fails ✗ |
|---|---|
| Bull2021a (+0.36) — rapid rise | Bear2022 (−0.46) — sustained bear trend |
| Bear2021 (+1.13) — sharp crash | Bull2023 (−0.15) — sustained bull trend |
| Bull2021b (+0.37) — post-crash bull | Chop2025 (−0.24) — low-vol sideways |
| Chop2024a (+0.13) — medium vol |  |
| Bull2024b (+1.64) — breakout bull |  |
| Bear2025 (+0.49) — 2025 bear |  |

Pattern: VWAP mean reversion works when price makes **sharp moves and then snaps back**
(high-vol reversals). It fails during **sustained directional trends** (price drifts away from
4H VWAP for weeks → signal fires repeatedly → each "reversion" trade enters into continuation).

| Cost regime | Sharpe | Commentary |

| 0bps gross | +1.155 | Strong underlying signal |

At maker rates (5bps RT, OKX Tier 1), SOL 1H shows +0.615 Sharpe. Unlike the 5m maker attempt
(fill rate 97% = no selectivity), at 1H the signal fires 2.9×/day and a limit order placed at
close[i] on 1H bars has meaningful fill selection — the 1H bar's range is wide enough that
high/low often doesn't touch the limit price on the next bar.
